## Graphical Solutions

Graphical solution involves drawing two equations on the same graph. The point where the lines intersect is the solution that satisfies both equations.

### Steps

1. Rewrite each equation in the form `y = mx + c`.
2. Plot both lines on the same graph.
3. Identify the intersection point.
4. The coordinates of the intersection are the solution values of the variables.

### Example

Solve the system:

        y = 2x + 1 \\
        y = -x + 4

* Step 1: Plot \( y = 2x + 1 \)  
  - When \( x = 0 \), \( y = 1 \) → point (0, 1)  
  - When \( x = 1 \), \( y = 3 \) → point (1, 3)

* Step 2: Plot \( y = -x + 4 \)  
  - When \( x = 0 \), \( y = 4 \) → point (0, 4)  
  - When \( x = 2 \), \( y = 2 \) → point (2, 2)

* Step 3: Draw both lines on the graph and find the intersection → (1, 3)
