## Graphical Representation

### Number Line

- **Open circle (○)** for `<` or `>` (value not included)  
- **Filled circle (●)** for `≤` or `≥` (value included)  
- **Arrow or shaded region** shows the direction or interval of the solution

---

### Example: Represent \( x \leq 3 \)

- Draw a **filled circle** at 3  
- Draw an **arrow** extending to the **left**

## Compound Inequalities


Compound inequalities combine **two conditions**:

- **"And"**: both conditions must be true (intersection of values)
- **"Or"**: either condition can be true (union of values)

---

### Example: Solve \( -2 < x \leq 3 \)

- x is **greater than -2** (not included)  
- x is **less than or equal to 3** (included)  

#### On a number line:
- **Open circle** at -2  
- **Filled circle** at 3  
- **Shaded region** in between
